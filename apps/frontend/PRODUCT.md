# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Finance and accounting teams, department heads, and executives reviewing and approving invoices. Users work in professional environments where clarity, efficiency, and trustworthiness are paramount. They need to quickly understand invoice status, make approval decisions, and maintain an audit trail of actions taken.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered invoice approval system that streamlines the approval workflow for organizations. Users submit invoice requests in natural language, the AI extracts structured data (amount, purpose, department), and the invoices move through approval chains (HOD → CEO) before payment processing. Success means fast, clear approvals with minimal friction and maximum transparency.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, precise, intentional. Professional without coldness. Built for experts who value clarity over decoration. Quiet confidence: the interface gets out of the way and lets the work happen.

Three words: **Precise, Trustworthy, Efficient**

Reference: Linear's dashboard aesthetic—high contrast, intentional hierarchy, understated interactions, deep focus on usability.

## Anti-references

- Overly colorful SaaS dashboards with gradients and decorative elements
- Skeuomorphic or heavily textured UIs
- Aggressive animations or micro-interactions
- Slanted/skewed buttons or typographic tricks
- Cluttered tables with excessive columns or information density
- Bright, saturated status colors that feel cheap or aggressive

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — every element has a purpose; nothing is added for visual interest alone
2. **Contrast as hierarchy** — use white/black and weight shifts to guide attention, not color or size alone
3. **Muted sophistication** — colors exist but don't scream; status indicators are refined and professional
4. **Dark mode parity** — the experience is equally strong in light and dark modes, not an afterthought
5. **Intentional whitespace** — breathing room between sections creates rhythm and reduces cognitive load

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Support reduced motion preferences (no forced animations)
- Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for UI components)
- Status information conveyed by more than color alone (icons, text labels)
- Keyboard navigation fully supported
- Screen reader friendly (semantic HTML, ARIA where needed)
